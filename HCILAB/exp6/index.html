Here's a breakdown of HTML structure and CSS styling demonstrate the Gestalt principles:

1. Similarity Play/Pause buttons: They share the same shape, color (.similar class), and visual style. This signals their relatedness and function to the user.
2. Proximity Checkboxes and task labels: Located close together, users naturally perceive them as connected items. Image editor tools: The "Crop", "Rotate", and "Resize" buttons are grouped in close proximity, conveying a relationship as a set of tools.
3. Closure Cards and sections: Although subtle, the boxes around cards and sections suggest complete shapes, even if the borders aren't fully "closed". This helps users recognize them as distinct units of information.
4. Continuation Placeholder in the search bar: Though the search bar has a background, the fainter placeholder text implies a line that could continue further to the right, leading the user in the direction they should type.
5. Figure-Ground Cards, sections, search bar: Each has a background color that distinguishes it from the main page background, instantly drawing the user's focus to the content. Image editor: The image itself is the figure against the tool section's background. This separation lets the user concentrate on editing actions.
6. Symmetry & Order Overall layout: Content is placed within a central container, creating a sense of balance. Card alignment: Cards are spaced symmetrically, providing a sense of organization and calmness. Tool buttons: Evenly spaced and consistently styled buttons create order and predictability.
